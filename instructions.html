Instructions

Dear , please read the instructions carefully before beginning the experiment.

About the Experiment

The experiment aims to test the usability and efficiency of 2 separated Copy & Paste techniques: AutoComPaste & Traditional Ctrl+C/V.

It is therefore necessary to familiarise you, the participant, with the interface, the techniques and the experiment.

The Inteface

The interface used for this experiment has 2 major sections: the Left & the Right sections.

On the Left section, it displays important information on a particular trial that you are working on.

On the Right section, it presents a few windows containing text sources as well as the Text Editor Window which you enter the target text into.

Important information to take note:

  • Technique: Whether you should be applying AutoComPaste or the Traditional Ctrl+C/V technique for a given trial.
  • Granuality: Whether the target text appears in the form of a Phrase, Sentence or Paragraph. (Hint: The texts will be highlighted in yellow as shown in the diagram below)
  • Source Window: Whether to expect the target text to appear from within the same window or across multiple window.s
  • Trial: The current Trial No.

  • Stimuli: This prompts you with the target text . Locate it, and apply the technique instructed and enter the text into the Text Editor Window .


  1. Read the instructions carefully.
  2. Keep in mind the technique to apply.
  3. When you are in the Text Editor Window , apply either AutoComPaste or Traditional Ctrl+C/V technique as instructed in Step 2.
  4. After completing the trial, click Next Task to continue.

AutoComPaste

AutoComPaste is a new technique that resides within the Text Editor Window . It replaces the need for traditional copying & pasting technique, by reading and analyzing all text in windows that are currently opened on your computer. To initialise the program, start with typing the text you want to copy the Text Editor Window and a dropdown list of matching texts will appear. You will navigate with the up/down arrow keys on your keyboard and enter to select the line of text you want.

Traditional (Ctrl-C/V) Keyboard Shortcuts

This method requires you to open your source and highlight text from its window. While the text is selected, press the shortcut keys on your keyboard: Cmd/Ctrl + C to copy the text & Cmd/Ctrl + V to paste the text. Remember to paste the text into the Text Editor Window .

The Experiment

There are a total of 108 trials for this experiment.

It is split into 3 blocks with 36 trials each.

For each block, you have to perform 18 trials on each technique and a 30-seconds break will be given in between each set.
